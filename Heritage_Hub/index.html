THE NATIONAL MUSEUM OF EGYPTIAN CIVILIZATION

General Information

National Museum of Egyptian Civilization is a major museum in Cairo that showcases around 50,000 artifacts covering Egypt’s history from prehistoric times to the present. Located in Old Cairo overlooking Ain El Sira Lake, it features a wide range of items including manuscripts, paintings, and antiquities. The museum officially opened on April 3, 2021, marked by the famous Pharaohs’ Golden Parade, when 22 royal mummies were moved there from the Egyptian Museum.

SHARM EL SHEIKH MUSEUM

General Information

The Sharm El Sheikh Museum is the first Egyptian antiquities museum in South Sinai. Opened in 2020 on Airport Road, it covers an area of 190,000 square meters and displays approximately 5,200 artifacts showcasing ancient Egyptian civilization and the heritage of Sinai.
Opening Date: Inaugurated by the President of the Republic in 2020, following the completion of construction that began in 2003 and was interrupted several times.
Exhibition Theme: The museum aims to present the history of Egyptian civilization, the relationship between humans and the environment, and the development of sciences and crafts. It includes taxidermied animals (cats, scarabs, and a crocodile) from the Saqqara area.

The Egyptian Museum

General Information

The Egyptian Museum in Cairo, located near Tahrir Square, is one of the world’s most famous museums. Established in 1835, it was relocated several times, from Azbakeya Garden to the Citadel, then to Bulaq by Auguste Mariette, before moving to Giza due to flooding. Its current building was opened in 1902 by Gaston Maspero during the reign of Khedive Abbas Helmy II, and it houses over 180,000 artifacts.

The Grand Egyptian Museum (GEM)

General Information

The Grand Egyptian Museum is a new cultural museum located near Giza, designed as a modern landmark for showcasing royal artifacts and treasures from the Pharaonic era. It features a large collection of statues, sarcophagi, jewelry and the complete Tutankhamun collection in a contemporary exhibition space.

The Luxor Museum

General Information

Alexandria National Museum

General Information

Alexandria National Museum is a major museum in Egypt that opened in 2003 in an Italian-style palace on Fouad Street. It contains more than 1,800 artifacts illustrating the history of Alexandria from the Pharaonic period through the Ptolemaic, Roman, Byzantine, Islamic, and modern eras. The collection was gathered from leading institutions such as Egyptian Museum, Coptic Museum, and Museum of Islamic Art, along with artifacts recovered from underwater sites. The museum is arranged across three floors, moving chronologically from ancient Egyptian artifacts in the basement to Greco-Roman pieces on the ground floor and later Byzantine, Islamic, and modern exhibits on the upper floor, featuring notable works such as statues of Isis, marble portraits, coins, and a statue of Venus..
