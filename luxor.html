The Luxor Museum

General Information

The Luxor Museum is a cultural museum located on the Nile Corniche in Luxor, displaying artifacts from the Theban temples and the Valley of the Kings. Opened in 1975, it is renowned for its carefully curated New Kingdom collection, including statues, reliefs and the reconstructed royal barque of Tutankhamun. The museum's simple gallery design helps visitors follow Luxor's archaeological story from the temple builders to the height of pharaonic civilization.
